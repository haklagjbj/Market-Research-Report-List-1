<p><p>High strength aluminum alloys are categorized into two main types: expandable alloys and casting alloys. Expandable alloys, including those used in aerospace and automotive applications, offer enhanced strength-to-weight ratios and are designed for structural integrity under stress. Casting alloys, on the other hand, are engineered for versatility in manufacturing and complex shapes, providing good fluidity and resistance to wear. Both types serve critical roles across industries, significantly contributing to advancements in lightweight design and performance.</p></p>
