<p><p>The trauma fixation product market comprises various internal and external fixation types. Internal fixation includes plates, screws, nails, and other devices used to stabilize fractured bones within the body. Plates provide surface support; screws secure bone fragments; nails are inserted into marrow cavities to align fractures; and other internal devices address specific needs. External fixation involves stabilizing fractures with devices attached to the bone externally, offering adjustable support and alignment during the healing process without requiring invasive surgery.</p></p>
