<p><p>Printed Circuit Board (PCB) drilling and routing are essential manufacturing processes that involve creating precise holes and pathways on PCBs to facilitate electrical connections. Drilling is primarily used for creating vias, which are necessary for connecting different layers of the board. Routing, on the other hand, involves cutting the PCB into its desired shape and creating traces that connect various components. These processes are crucial for achieving the needed functionality and reliability in electronic devices.</p><p>The market for PCB drilling and routing is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for electronics in sectors such as automotive, telecommunications, and consumer electronics. As technologies evolve, the complexity of PCBs also rises, leading to advancements in drilling and routing technologies to accommodate smaller components and tighter tolerances.
