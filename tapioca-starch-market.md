<p><p>The tapioca starch market is primarily divided into two types: original starch and modified starch. Original starch is derived directly from tapioca roots and is used for its natural thickening and binding properties in food and industrial applications. Modified starch, on the other hand, undergoes processing to enhance specific characteristics such as stability, viscosity, and shelf-life. This makes modified starch suitable for diverse uses, including in food products, pharmaceuticals, and chemical industries, catering to varied consumer demands.</p></p>
